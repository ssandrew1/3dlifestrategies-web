Strategic Action Items for 2026

A comprehensive roadmap for building fitness, legacy leadership, and deeper faith integration across Body, Mind, and Spirit dimensions.

Priority Actions by Domain
🏃

Body

Physical Foundation & Fitness

  • Request a complimentary fitness assessment at your gym to establish baseline cardiovascular and strength measurements
  • Structure a 3-day per week exercise program combining two resistance training days and one dedicated cardio day
  • Join your wife for pickleball weekly as a social fitness activity that combines exercise with relationship investment
  • Schedule a "Fit-for-50" medical checkup covering cardiovascular health, blood pressure, cholesterol, and testosterone levels
  • Research age-appropriate supplementation with your physician, focusing on Vitamin D3, Magnesium, Omega-3, and Creatine
  • Implement a tax-season fitness contingency plan to maintain minimum viable workouts during Q1 peak periods
  • Begin a walk-to-jog progression using a structured 8-week Couch-to-5K style program for safe running re-entry
💡

Mind

Learning & Leadership Development

  • Identify three targeted podcasts aligned with your goals: fitness science, Christian business leadership, and family legacy mentorship
  • Block intentional "learning time" on your calendar—20–30 minutes minimum during commutes or evening hours for focused content consumption
  • Develop a structured son onboarding plan that emphasizes character development and faith-based leadership alongside technical training
  • Document your business operating system using EOS framework to prepare for sustainable succession and your son's integration
  • Study the "Caleb Model" in Joshua 14 to develop your personal "second half" vision and mountain-sized purpose for retirement years
  • Create a formal mentorship covenant with your son outlining spiritual, professional, and relational objectives for your working relationship
  • Establish a monthly leadership reflection practice reviewing how Biblical principles are being applied in business decisions and employee interactions
✝

Spirit

Purpose, Faith & Legacy

  • Develop a personal Kingdom Legacy Statement articulating your vision for how God wants to work through you in your second half
  • Establish a specific confession and accountability rhythm with your C12 group or trusted peer for addressing pride and control patterns
  • Create a daily surrender prayer practice—brief morning commitment of business, family, and outcomes to God's sovereignty
  • Engage C12 resources to formalize legacy planning documentation that reflects your Christian values and long-term community vision
  • Build structured father-son discipleship conversations with your son, integrating faith, integrity, and work-as-calling discussions
  • Study biblical figures of faithful aging—Caleb, Moses, Paul, and Anna—to develop a theology of purposeful second-half stewardship
  • Implement scheduled Sabbath and silence rhythms to create margin for spiritual replenishment amid caregiving and leadership demands
Books to Read
Body
Younger Next Year: Live Strong, Fit, and Sexy — Until You're 80 and Beyond
Chris Crowley & Henry S. Lodge, MD
Co-written by a physician and a patient, this book builds a research-backed case for why the body responds dramatically differently to exercise in your second half of life. The authors explain biological mechanisms showing how consistent aerobic and strength training can effectively reverse aging by 10–15 years physiologically. Their direct, practical tone suits a results-oriented CPA mindset perfectly.
Mind
The Common Rule: Habits of Purpose for an Age of Distraction
Justin Whitmel Earley
Written by a Christian attorney and business professional, this book presents a framework for structuring daily and weekly habits around what truly matters—faith, family, work, and health. Earley offers four daily habits and four weekly habits designed to align life rhythms with deeply held values. For someone navigating tax season chaos and family caregiving, this framework intentionally integrates competing demands rather than treating them as separate.
Mind
Atomic Habits: An Easy & Proven Way to Build Good Habits & Break Bad Ones
James Clear
Clear's central thesis is that lasting behavior change comes from systems—tiny, consistent 1% daily improvements stacked into automatic routines—rather than dramatic willpower efforts. His identity-based approach ("I am a person who exercises" rather than "I want to exercise") is particularly relevant as you transition from inactivity to consistent fitness. The implementation tools survive disruptions like tax season and caregiving demands.
Spirit
Halftime: Moving from Success to Significance
Bob Buford
Buford's framework for men transitioning from "first half" success (career achievement, accumulation) to "second half" significance (contribution, kingdom purpose) speaks directly to your season. He provides practical tools for developing a documented Kingdom Legacy Statement and aligning your business and community vision with spiritual calling. This resource will intersect powerfully with your C12 work and retirement visioning.
