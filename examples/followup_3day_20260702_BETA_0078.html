Your Action Items

A man of genuine character called to ministry, ready to invest in personal renewal while honoring his calling and family.

⚡ Body — 3/5
🧠 Mind — 3/5
✝ Spirit — 4/5
Priority Actions by Domain
🏃
Body
  • Schedule a physician checkup and baseline fitness assessment within 30 days before intensifying exercise
  • Protect a consistent 3x/week gym schedule with early morning time blocks before ministry demands begin
  • Schedule pickleball with your wife 1–2 times per week as both cardio and marriage investment
  • Establish one wind-down sleep anchor habit such as no screens 30 minutes before bed or short prayer time
  • Request basic bloodwork for Vitamin D, Magnesium, and B12 deficiencies before starting any supplement regimen
  • Begin with 2x/week gym sessions for the first 3 weeks, then progress to 3x/week to avoid early injury or burnout
  • Practice the two-minute morning movement rule: five push-ups, stretching, or a brisk walk before checking messages
💡
Mind
  • Select 2–3 high-quality health and fitness podcasts and assign them to commute and gym time for zero-overhead learning
  • Schedule a 90-minute consultation with a certified financial planner to create a clear 12-month financial priority list
  • Identify and reach out to an accountability partner—a trusted pastoral peer, mentor, or coach—within the next 30 days
  • Research part-time or online seminary programs to keep advanced education dreams alive without immediate commitment
  • Commit to consuming one exercise science or nutrition podcast episode per week for 60 days to build foundational knowledge
  • Read at least one chapter per week from either Deep Work or The Automatic Millionaire to reclaim mental focus and financial clarity
  • Track body metrics beyond weight—energy levels, sleep quality, and mood—to measure real progress aligned with ministry capacity
✝
Spirit
  • Establish one protected family day or half-day per week where ministry demands are absolutely not permitted to intrude
  • Actively seek an older pastoral mentor through your denomination network or seminary contacts within 30 days
  • Commit to attending a minimum of two of your son's sporting events per month and record the memory
  • Conduct a quarterly "State of the Family" conversation with your wife to align vision, name costs, and renew marriage covenant
  • Study the biblical Sabbath theologically through Exodus 20, Deuteronomy 5, and Isaiah 58 to reframe rest as spiritual discipline
  • Journal weekly about one moment where family came first over ministry demand to train attention toward relational wins
  • Name specific fears (about your son, daughter's care, aging parents, financial pressure) in writing and anchor each to a biblical promise
Books to Read
Body
Younger Next Year
Chris Crowley & Henry S. Lodge, M.D.
A science-backed case for why consistent exercise in your forties is transformational. Explains the biology of aging and how the body responds remarkably well to regular strength and cardio work—perfectly aligned with your 12-month physical goal and deep need to understand the why.
Body
The Daniel Plan: 40 Days to a Healthier Life
Rick Warren, Dr. Daniel Amen & Dr. Mark Hyman
Uniquely integrates Christian faith with practical nutrition, fitness, and wellness guidance. Frames health as a spiritual discipline and emphasizes community—perfect for someone in ministry who wants health grounded in faith language and shared with his wife's involvement.
Mind
Deep Work
Cal Newport
Provides practical strategies for carving out periods of deep, uninterrupted focus even in a schedule full of demands. Directly relevant for someone whose work is constantly interrupt-driven and whose learning requires protected mental space.
Mind
The Automatic Millionaire
David Bach
Presents a simple system for financial security that does not require complex knowledge. Particularly suited to someone whose schedule leaves no margin for active financial management. Addresses debt, retirement, and building margin over time.
Spirit
Strengthening the Soul of Your Leadership
Ruth Haley Barton
Writes directly to ministry leaders about the critical importance of inner spiritual formation. Explores how even gifted leaders become dangerously depleted without intentional soul care. Speaks directly to the tension between serving God and protecting family.
Spirit
Boundaries
Dr. Henry Cloud & Dr. John Townsend
Reframes limits as spiritually healthy and biblically grounded. Specifically addresses how high-agreeable, service-oriented individuals struggle to protect their own time and energy. Provides both theological permission and practical tools.
