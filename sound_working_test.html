CURRICULUM OVERVIEW

3D Life Strategies: Integrating Gifts, Talents, and Action

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the "3D Life Strategies" framework, focusing on the holistic development of an individual's Body, Mind, and Spirit.

I. The 3D Life Strategies Framework

The 3D Life Strategies approach is built upon the premise that every individual possesses God-given gifts and talents. The framework operates through a specific process of personal evolution:

The 3D Process

  • Discovery: Identifying one's personality, character, and KSAs (Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities).
  • Decision: Making the choice to move forward with these identified traits.
  • Development: Working with a coach to establish SMART Goals that turn latent abilities into active development.

II. Biblical Foundations of Action and Stewardship

Knowledge without action is "vanity." The following biblical principles form the foundation for using one's talents:

  • James 1:22: Instruction to be "doers of the word" rather than just hearers.
  • James 4:17: States that failing to do the right thing when one knows it is considered sin.
  • John 13:17: Notes that a blessing comes from doing what is known, not just knowing it.

Stewardship and Accountability

Stewardship vs. Ownership: Individuals are stewards, not owners, of their skills. Careers and hobbies are property entrusted by God to be managed for His kingdom.

The Risk of Inaction: Fear is the primary enemy of action. As seen in the Parable of the Talents, burying a talent due to insecurity is a waste of divine opportunity.

III. KSAs vs. Spiritual Gifts

Feature Natural Talents / KSAs Spiritual Gifts
Source Genetic inheritance and environment The Holy Spirit (at spiritual birth)
Purpose Personal success or societal benefit To edify the Church and glorify God

The Intersection Matrix

Strategic Planning + Administration: Resulting in leading church projects or non-profits.
Empathy + Exhortation: Resulting in counseling or mentoring.
Writing + Teaching: Resulting in explaining theology or devotionals.

IV. Identification and Discovery

The "expert's curse" involves believing that because a task is easy for you, it is not a skill.

The Three-Circle Alignment

To find the "sweet spot" for action, one should look for the overlap of three areas:

  • Ability (The "Can"): What you are naturally good at.
  • Affinity (The "Want"): What you care about.
  • Affirmation (The "Call"): What others and spiritual fruit indicate.
